---
title: "Simple Rules"
description: "Rich structure rarely needs rich rules — usually a few local ones, iterated."
date: "2026-06-11"
tags: ["systems", "emergence"]
---

Conway's Life has four rules and produces gliders. Boids have three and produce flocks. The
graph's clustering has essentially one: *adopt the most common label among your neighbors,
repeat.* Iterate that over the chaos of links and communities appear.

The lesson for a note system: don't over-engineer the taxonomy. Provide the simple rule (links)
and let [[Emergence]] and [[Self-Organization]] do the rest.
