title: "Maps of Content"
description: "Hand-made index notes that grow over a cluster after it has formed."

---

A Map of Content (MOC) is a note whose job is to point at other notes — a table of contents you
write *after* a topic has accreted, not before. It's the deliberate counterpart to the graph's
automatic clustering.

MOCs pair with [[Atomic Notes]] and [[Zettelkasten]], and they're where [[The Serendipity Engine]]
deposits the connections worth keeping. When [[Self-Organization]] produces a cluster, a MOC names it.
