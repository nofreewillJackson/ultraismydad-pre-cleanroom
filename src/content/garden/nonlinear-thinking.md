---
title: "Nonlinear Thinking"
description: "Thought doesn't arrive in outline order — and a tool shouldn't force it to."
date: "2026-06-09"
tags: ["thinking", "adhd"]
---

Ideas arrive out of order, sideways, mid-shower. Forcing them into a tidy outline loses the
shape of how they actually connect. A linked graph lets the structure stay latent until it's
ready to show itself — which is the whole point of [[Emergence]].

For [[Note-taking for ADHD]] this matters double: the bottleneck is [[Working Memory]], and a
graph offloads the "how does this relate to that" question onto something you can see. The
payoff is [[The Serendipity Engine]] — rediscovering a connection you'd forgotten you could make.
