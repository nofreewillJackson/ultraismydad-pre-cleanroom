The slip-box is the ancestor of every digital garden: each idea on its own slip, addressed and
linked to others, so the collection becomes a network you can converse with. The discipline is
[[Atomic Notes]] — one idea per note — and emergent [[Maps of Content]] instead of top-down folders.

Luhmann credited the box, not himself, for the output. The structure did the remembering — which
is exactly the [[Working Memory]] argument.
